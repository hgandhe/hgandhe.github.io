Which labor pain management option is best for you?

Choose Your Labor Pain Relief is a patient decision aid that was developed to assist pregnant women in determining which labor pain management options are best for them. This decision tool provides educational information regarding the different pain management options, provides a personalized recommendation, and informs about the potential next steps to take.

Disclaimer:

Tool is for informational purposes only and does not substitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Please consult a medical professional for medical problems or concerns.


Pharmaceutical Options

Please read through the following sections to learn about the pharmaceutical pain management options for labor.

Nitrus Oxide

What is it:

Also known as laughing gas, nitrus oxide is an odorless and tasteless gas that is often used for pain relief in many different minor medical procedures. For labor, nitrus oxide dulls pain and reduces anxiety so that the pain is easier to manage. This method is more commonly used outside of the United States in countries such as Canada, Sweden, Australia, and the United Kingdom.

How is it administered:

Nitrus oxide is inhaled through a mask as needed throughout labor and delivery. The mask can be removed and placed back as needed by the mother. It is best used when inhaling before contractions begin. Nitrus oxide works and wears off quickly, so it is best used as needed throughout the duration of labor to continue providing pain relief.

Effectiveness:

In terms of pain relief, nitrus oxide is considered a weak anesthetic, so it does not eliminate pain but only dulls it. Thus, it may be benifial to consider using nitrus oxide in combination with a non-pharmaceutical or natural pain managment option for more effective pain relief if needed.

Side effects and risks:

Nitrus oxide does not affect the progress of labor, the baby, or breastfeeding because it does not interfere with the release and function of labor hormones. It may cause dizziness, nausea, or vomiting, but the sensation will wear off minutes after stopping the medication.

Systemic Analgesia (Opiates)

What is it:

Also known as opiates, systemic analgesia acts on the entire body to reduce pain everywhere. This allows for a reduced awareness of pain and a calming effect, but it does not cause loss of consciousness.

How is it administered:

The medication is generally given through an injection or an IV. The medication cannot be given before labor because it can affect the baby, so it must be given after labor begins. The medication is released in doses which can either be administered by a health care professional or self administered. Self administration allow the the patient to press a button to release a dose as needed. The IV will not allow for doses to be released too close to one another and the amount released is limited so it is not possible to give yourself too much.

Effectiveness:

This method is most effective in reducing pain during the early and middle stages of labor. The patient's vitals will need to be monitored to ensure that it is safe to continue administering doses.

Side effects and risks:

Side effects can include nausea, vomiting, and drowsiness. Other medications can be given to reduce nausea and vomiting if needed. This method can also cause the baby to be drowsy, making breast feeding difficult for the first few hours.

Regional Pain Relief (Epidural)

What is it:

This method is a regional anesthesia that blocks sensation below the waist, but allows the mother to remain awake and alert. There are two types of epidurals: Standard Epidural and Combined Spinal-Epidural. The Combined Spinal-Epidural allows for some sensation in the lower body where as the Standard Epidural allows for less sensation in the lower body. Epidurals may cause restricted or minial movement, and walking around may not be possible. Epidurals must be given during the first stages of labor to be most effective. However, it can be given later in labor if needed.

How is it administered:

The medication is administered through a IV in the lower back. The needle is inserted around the spinal nerves. Then a soft tube, called a catheter, is inserted. The catheter is how the medication is administered. Pain relief takes approximately 30 minutes to being working. The medicine dosage can be increased in the case of an emergency caesarean.

Effectiveness:

This method is the most commonly used method for pain relief and is the most effective in relieving labor pains. Having an epidural is benificial if labor is long or slow because it can allow the mother to rest and conserve energy while the cervix dilates so they have energy for when it is time to push.

Side effects and risks:

Epidurals are generally safe. However, some common side effects that could occur are headache, itching, lowered blood pressure, difficulty uriniating, slowed progression of labor, and may cause difficulty breast feeding. Risks on the baby are low and serious complications are rare.



Non-Pharmaceutical Options

Please read through the following sections to learn about the natural pain management methods.

Hydrotherapy

What is it:

Also known as Water Immersion, this method involves immersion into warm water. This method can be used during any part of labor up until delivery begins. Hydrotherapy is different from water birth because water birth is when the baby is delivered underwater in a special tub. Hydrotherapy is only used during labor and not delivery. However, hydrotherapy and waterbirth can be used together for low risk pregnancies.

Effectiveness:

This method can be calming, reduce anxiety, and reduce stress. The water allows the pelvic tissue to be more elastic, easing pain during contractions. This method also reduces pain of contractions because the pressure difference between the abdominal cavity and the water allows for muscles to be relaxed. The water allows for ease with breathing and changing positions. Movement is easier with hydrotherpay because the mother is supported by water, and physical movement allows labor to progress. Hydrotherpay can be a good method to delay the need for pain medication.

Side effects and risks:

Risks for this method are low. However, good candidates for hydrotherpay are generally individuals with low risk, full term pregnancy with a single baby. Individuals with higher risk pregnancies may choose to opt for a different method because hydrotherapy may make it more difficult to monitor the babys vitals.

Controlled Breathing

What is it:

Controlled breathing consists of several breathing patterns that are intended to help with labor and delivery. Some common methods include belly breathing which involves taking slow deep breaths to fill your abdomen with air and exhaling slowly. This is done between contractions. Another common technique is to follow a pattern when breathing. This method can be inhaling enough to fill the chest or breathing deeply. The goal is to find a pattern of breathing that is calming and breathing should not be too fast causing shortness of breath or lightheaded. A common patterned breathing technique is to do one long deep inhale and exhale with two short pants and one long blow. This is done during contractions to handle the pain.

Effectiveness:

Using patterned breathing methods during contractions can provide a sense of control. Steady breathing can also be calming during labor. Using patterned breathing can give increased oxygen to the baby and the mother.

Side effects and risks:

There are no risks while using this method and it causes no harm to the baby. However, make sure to practice your breathing method before going into labor to ensure it will not cause you to feel light headed or short of breath. Also, this method can also be used in conjunction with other pain management methods to provide more pain relief.

Massage

What is it:

Having your partner or a support person massage along the back, shoulders, feet, and hands during labor can ease pain. The ideal positions for massaging are leaning forward on a birthing ball, sitting in a chair, or resting on a bed. It may be helpful to practice massages before labor to determine what massages you like and what is the most calming.

Effectiveness:

This method may cause temporary relief and relax muscles and joints. This is especially helpful for back soreness or aches. Massaging can also reduce swelling, anxiety, and stress, and provide emotional support. This method improves blood circulation, which benifits the mother and the baby.

Side effects and risks:

If you are using massage oils make sure that they are pregnancy and labor safe. Otherwise, there are no side effects for this pain managment method, and this method can be used in conjunction with other pain managment options.

Positioning

What is it:

Certain standing or sitting positions can allow for relief during labor. Positions such as squatting, kneeling, lunging, and leaning forward can help with lower back pain. Positions such as rocking on a chair or on a birthing ball and waling can help ease contractions.

Effectiveness:

This method relaxes muscles and reduces anxiety. This method can also make contractions more bearable and allow you to feel in control. This method can be used in conjunction with other pain managment options that don't constrict movement.

Side effects and risks:

Certain squatting or sitting positions can cause more perineal trama and greater blood loss.

Hot-Cold Therapy

What is it:

A hot pack and cold pack can be on different areas of the body throughout labor and delivery. For applying heat a warm washcloth, a sock with rice, or a store bought heating pad can be used. For applying cold either ice, a washcloth under cold water, or store bought cold pack can be used.

Effectiveness:

The hot pack is used to relax muscles, and the cold pack is used to provide a distraction to the nerves. This method can provide relief in various parts of the body because the hot or cold packs can be placed anywhere.

Side effects and risks:

When using this pain managment method, be careful to make sure the tempurature of the packs are not too hot or too cold to the point where they case burns or stress the skin. This pain managment method can be used in conjunction with other pain managment options for more pain relief.

Aromatherapy

What is it:

This method involves using essential oils to provide comfort, reduce anxiety and stress. Essential oils can be used by smelling a small drop of the oil on cotton or using a diffuser to spread the smell around the room.

Effectiveness:

There is no evidence that this pain managment method provides pain relief. However, this method may cause relaxation and reduce anxiety. This method may work better in conjunction with other pain managment options.

Side effects and risks:

There are no side effects for this pain managment method. It is important to choose a smell that you like because your sense of smell can be more sensitive during labor, and too much essential oils could cause nausea. Essenstial oils should not be used on the skin.
